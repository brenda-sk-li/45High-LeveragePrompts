1. Control & Structure (The Architect)

Set the rules, role, and formal output requirements.

Defining the Role

Act as a [specific role, e.g., CTO].

Setting Length Limit

Limit the response to a maximum of [number] words/sentences.

Demanding Specific Format

Output the result as a Markdown table with columns: [A, B, C].

Requiring Steps

Show your work step-by-step before the final answer.

Pattern Replication

Follow the exact formatting and structure of the example I provide.

Setting the Tone

The tone must be neutral, professional, and direct.

Excluding Content

Exclude all references to [specific topic/concept].

Forcing Concision

Provide only the core information; eliminate all introductions.

Defining Constraints

The solution must strictly adhere to the [specific constraint].

Preventing Preamble

Do not write an introduction or conclusion. Start immediately with the first point.

2. Quality & Clarity (The Editor)

Demand better logic, precise formatting, and specific thinking.

Clarify First

Ask clarifying questions if unclear; never proceed with assumptions.

Plan First (Iterative)

Walk me through your plan before implementing the final solution.

Critiquing Own Work

Identify the two weakest points or risks of your proposed solution.

Seeking Alternatives

Generate three alternative solutions to this problem.

Simplifying Complexity

Explain this concept using an analogy a [age/profession] would understand.

Requiring Detail

Provide a comprehensive, in-depth analysis of [topic].

Forcing Brevity

Give me the shortest possible correct answer.

Preventing Generic Output

Provide original, non-obvious ideas. Exclude all common knowledge.

Challenging an Answer

Argue against your own main point (Devil's Advocate).

Distinguishing Fact vs. Opinion

Clearly distinguish between facts from the provided data versus your general recommendations.

Image Descriptions

Add text descriptions for technical diagrams/complex images before sharing.

3. Error & Loop Correction (The Reset Button)

Break loops, force immediate execution proof, and initiate precise changes.

Breaking a Loop (Nuclear)

STOP. Start fresh. Respond with only [the core answer].

Recalibrating Tone

Cut the fluff. / Less cheerleading, more analysis.

Targeted Edit

Only change the introduction paragraph, keep everything else intact.

Explaining the Error

This response is too [vague/long]. Explain what went wrong.

Requesting Specific Change

Change *only* the tone of the conclusion to be more optimistic.

Forcing Iteration

Improve the clarity of the second bullet point.

Confirming Edits

Show me the revised version with the changes highlighted.

Switching Context

Ignore all previous context. New independent task: [task].

Preventing "Theater"

Show me the actual output, do not accept 'I have completed it.'

Continuing Response

Continue the response from where you left off.

4. Hallucination & Verification (The Validator)

Force factual verification, mathematical justification, and honesty.

Detecting Hallucination

STOP. Cite your source or admit this is a guess.

Challenging a Claim

How confident are you in this answer? (0-100%).

Demanding Honesty

If you don't know, explicitly say "I do not know" rather than guessing.

Forcing Verification

Search for verification before answering. Do not rely on internal knowledge.

Demanding Proof/Source

For all factual claims, cite your source or state 'uncited.' (e.g., Cite your source for that 47% figure)

Preventing Fabricated Citations

Only cite sources you can verify exist; do not invent placeholders.

Enforcing Grounding

Base your answer only on the provided text.

Math Justification

For all calculations, provide step-by-step to show how the math is done.

Reverse Engineering

All numerical results must be justifiable and can be reverse engineered.

Resetting Context/Memory

Forget everything except the following prompt: [Re-state the core instruction].

Preventing Context Drift

Before answering, summarize the three key decisions we made earlier.

Re-Injecting Critical Data

You appear to have lost [Data]. Here it is again: [Data]. Use only this.

5. Behavioral Flaws & Iteration (The Director)

Force multi-step thinking and actively control the pace of execution.

Stopping Moralizing

Just give me the facts. Omit all safety preambles and caveats.

Countering Superficiality

Identify the critical path and weigh the three most important factors in this analysis.

Correcting Hallucination

Erase the false claim and regenerate the answer based only on my previous statements.

Confirm Understanding (Pause)

Confirm your understanding of the task before generating the response.

Forced Checkpoint (Slow Down)

Stop after the [outline/first step] and wait for my approval before proceeding.

Phase Planning

Break this complex request into phases: [Phase A], then [Phase B].
